# RehabNet: AI Models and AR/VR Documentation

This document outlines the core Artificial Intelligence (AI) models and Augmented Reality / Virtual Reality (AR/VR) technologies powering the RehabNet Parkinson's Rehabilitation platform.

---

## 1. Artificial Intelligence (AI) Models

The RehabNet platform uses a modular AI architecture located in the FastAPI backend (`backend/ai/`). The system relies on real-time signal processing and heuristic classification, designed to be scalable to more complex Machine Learning (ML) models in the future.

### 1.1 Tremor Detection Model (`tremor_detector.py`)
This model processes the raw 3-axis accelerometer data (`ax`, `ay`, `az`) streamed from the ESP32 wearable hardware over Wi-Fi. It is specifically tuned to identify the resting tremor footprint of Parkinson's Disease.

* **Input Data:** Time-series arrays of X, Y, and Z physical acceleration, generated by the MPU6050 sensor at a sampling rate of ~50Hz.
* **Core Algorithm:** Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) via the `scipy.fft` module.
* **Processing Steps:**
  1. Consolidate the 3-axis data into a single magnitude vector: `sqrt(ax² + ay² + az²)`.
  2. Perform Mean Subtraction to eliminate the constant 1g force of Earth's gravity.
  3. Apply the FFT over a sliding window (e.g., 256 samples) to transition the data from the time domain into the frequency domain.
  4. Isolate the "Dominant Frequency" by finding the highest peak magnitude.
  5. Calculate the Root Mean Square (RMS) of the amplitude to determine the intensity of the physical shaking.
* **Classification Output:**
  * **Frequency (Hz):** Parkinson's resting tremors typically manifest strongly in the 4 Hz to 7 Hz range.
  * **Severity (Label):** Evaluates the RMS amplitude inside the 4-7Hz window and outputs a categorical label (`Low`, `Medium`, or `High`).

### 1.2 Movement Classification Model (`movement_classifier.py`)
This model analyzes the physical body mechanics of the patient during rehabilitation exercises (e.g., Arm Raises). It operates on coordinates extracted by the mobile phone's camera.

* **Input Data:** A JSON array of 33 normalized 3D joint landmarks (e.g., `leftWrist`, `rightShoulder`), continuously streamed from the Flutter frontend.
* **Core Algorithm:** Rule-based heuristics and trigonometric angle extraction. (Structured as an MVP to be easily swapped with a `RandomForestClassifier` using `scikit-learn` in later phases).
* **Processing Steps:**
  1. Identifies the bounding box constraints of the specific joints required for the active exercise (e.g., tracking the Y-axis delta between the Wrist and the Shoulder).
  2. Applies conditional logic measuring joint extension. For example, if `wrist_y` exceeds `shoulder_y` by a specific margin, the extension is considered successful.
  3. Extracts localized angle measurements using the Dot Product of the vectors between 3 joint nodes if complex joint analysis is required.
* **Classification Output:**
  * **Accuracy Percentage:** A 0-100% score for the specific repetition.
  * **Status:** `Correct`, `Incorrect`, or `Needs Improvement`.
  * **Live Feedback String:** Actionable instructions sent directly to the patient's screen (e.g., *"Raise your hand higher"*).

### 1.3 Gait Analyzer Model (`gait_analyzer.py` - Future Scope)
Designed to detect the phenomenon of "Freezing of Gait" (FoG) or postural instability.

* **Processing Concept:** Measures the standard deviation (variance) of the ankle's Y-trajectory or foot-mounted IMU acceleration to identify periods where cadence drops to zero while stepping intent remains, returning `Freezing detected` or `Unstable` pacing flags.

## 2. AR / VR Rehabilitation Mechanics

RehabNet utilizes the Flutter framework (`CustomPainter` and `google_mlkit_pose_detection`) to render Augmented Reality (AR) and Virtual Reality (VR) environments directly on the mobile device without relying on heavy game engines like Unity.

### 2.1 AR Hand Agility (`ar_exercise_screen.dart`)
This module provides Augmented Reality visual markers overlaid on a live camera feed to encourage broad movement and improve motor agility.

* **Computer Vision Tracking:** Utilizes **Google ML Kit Pose Detection**. It runs wholly on-device, processing the camera feed to generate the 33 skeletal landmarks. This avoids network latency and ensures the patient's camera feed never leaves the device.
* **Skeleton Overlay:** The `CustomPainter` API dynamically draws a neon "medical" skeleton over the patient's arms, precisely anchoring to the ML Kit coordinates.
* **AR Interaction Logic:**
  1. A virtual target (`_pulseAnim` glowing circle) is mathematically spawned at a random, normalized (0.0 to 1.0) coordinate on the screen.
  2. The Euclidean distance between the user's `leftWrist`/`rightWrist` landmarks and the target's center is calculated on every frame.
  3. When the wrist enters the target's radius threshold before the countdown timer expires, it registers as a "Hit," improving the patient's reaction time and accuracy metrics.

### 2.2 VR Hand Interaction (`vr_hand_screen.dart`)
This module creates an immersive 3D environment to improve depth perception, targeting, and rhythmic movement capability.

* **Pseudo-3D Rendering Engine:** Built entirely from scratch using Flutter's `Canvas` and `CustomPainter`.
* **Perspective Projection:**
  * The engine mathematically manipulates 2D coordinates to create the illusion of a 3D "tunnel."
  * Targets (spheres) are spawned at a distant "Z-depth" and scale up exponentially as their Z-index decreases to simulate approaching the user.
* **VR Interaction Logic:**
  * The patient uses the touchscreen (or potentially the ESP32 mapped as a gyroscope mouse equivalent) to move a "Virtual Hand Cursor" hovering in the foreground.
  * When an approaching 3D object reaches the Z-index of 0 (the interaction plane), the engine calculates the 2D bounding box intersection between the target and the hand cursor. Safe intercepts register as hits.
* **Cognitive Enhancements:** Provides audio metronomes (`audioplayers`) to assist patients with rhythmic auditory cueing, which has been shown to temporarily bypass basal ganglia deficits in Parkinson's patients.
